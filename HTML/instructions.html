Copy-Paste Converter Guide

Important: Some timezone abbreviations depend on Daylight Saving Time (DST). For example, PT may refer to either PST or PDT depending on the date, (e.g. PDT stands for Pacific Daylight Time and PST stands for Pacific Savings Time.) Because of this, you should enter the EXACT timezone abbreviation shown in the original source. Do not assume that a broad regional label like PT always means the same UTC offset.

This tool converts pasted times only when they follow the specific supported input formats and use a recognized timezone abbreviation or UTC offset with regards to time zone offsets. To get the most accurate result, enter the time exactly as it appears in the original source.

Supported Formats

1. Time first (Standard Formatting)

8 PM PST
8:30 PM JST
2030 UTC+8
0030 UTC+8

2. Timezone first (Reverse Formatting)

PST 8 PM
JST 8:30 PM
UTC+8 2030
UTC+8 0030

Accepted Time Formats

The converter accepts compact and standard numeric time formats.

8 08 800 0800 8:00 08:00

AM/PM Rules

  • With AM or PM, the input is read as 12-hour time (Standard).
  • Without AM or PM, the input is read as 24-hour time (Military).

Timezone Rules

  • Only the supported timezone abbreviations and UTC offset tokens will work.
  • E.g.: PST, EST, JST, PHT, UTC+8, UTC-5.
  • Full timezone names like Asia/Manila or Pacific Time will not work.
  • You should always detect your own timezone first before converting.

Examples That Work

8 PM PST
PST 8 PM
13:45 CET
CET 1345
2300 UTC+8
UTC+8 11:00 PM

Examples That Will Not Work

8 o'clock PST
Pacific Standard Time 8 PM
Manila 8 PM
8PM in Tokyo

For Ambiguous Timezones

There are certain timezones wherein they have different Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) offsets, but similar abbreviations. In these cases, the key-value mapping for these were changed to their specified continent/country. For example:

  • IST is ambiguous since it can mean Israel Standard Time and India Standard Time which have different UTC offsets, in this case, we specify them accordingly as: IST-ISRAEL and IST respectively.

Why Some Inputs Fail

The converter only checks against supported timezone abbreviations in the app via key-value pairing. It does not interpret full timezone names or region names by themselves, as it would make the list too long. That is why only abbreviations like PST will work, while phrases like Pacific Standard Time will not.
